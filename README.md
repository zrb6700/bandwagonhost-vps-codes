## How CN2 GIA Actually Works (Without the Marketing Speak)

Regular internet traffic between, say, Los Angeles and Shanghai gets shuffled across shared international routes. During peak hours — evenings in China — that shared infrastructure gets congested. Packet loss increases. Pages load slowly. Video calls break up.

CN2 GIA is China Telecom's premium tier routing. It uses dedicated infrastructure with much lower utilization, so your traffic skips the rush-hour pile-up. Real-world testing from the BandwagonHost community shows CN2 GIA routes averaging around 158ms latency from mainland China with minimal packet loss, even during peak evening hours.

CN2 GT (Global Transit) is the mid-tier option — noticeably better than standard routing, but not as premium as GIA. The standard 20G/40G/80G KVM plans use this routing.

Does this matter if you're not in China? Honestly, the over-engineered infrastructure still benefits everyone. Fewer hops, better routing decisions, more stable connections. Think of it like buying a car built for the Autobahn — you're in the suburbs, but it still handles better.
